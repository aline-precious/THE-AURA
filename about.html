About this platform

Why AttachSecure exists

Attachment theory — pioneered by John Bowlby and Mary Ainsworth — holds that the bonds we form with caregivers in early life create a template for how we relate to others throughout adulthood. These templates are not fixed.

AttachSecure is built on a single conviction: understanding your attachment style is not the end of the journey — it's the beginning. The destination is earned security: the capacity for stable, open, resilient connection that anyone can develop, regardless of their starting point.

What the research says

Studies in interpersonal neurobiology (Siegel, 2010) and attachment-based psychotherapy consistently show that secure attachment can be earned through:

  • Consistent self-reflection and narrative coherence — making sense of your own story
  • Corrective emotional experiences — relationships that contradict old patterns
  • Somatic and mindfulness-based practices that regulate the nervous system
  • Skilled therapeutic support from an attachment-informed clinician

The three roles this platform serves

The Individual Seeker

Someone doing personal work — not necessarily in a relationship, but committed to understanding their patterns and growing toward security.

The Connected Couple

Two partners using shared tools to understand their dynamic, communicate more effectively, and support each other's growth simultaneously.

The Licensed Therapist

A professional who uses the platform's data and tools as a complement to clinical work — tracking client progress and using the AI coach as between-session support.

A note on this tool

AttachSecure is not a replacement for therapy. The assessment is not a clinical diagnostic instrument. If you are experiencing significant distress, please seek support from a trained therapist.
